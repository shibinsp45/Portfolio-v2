How I think
about my work

01

Sketch ugly, decide fast

Pretty mockups slow teams down. I'd rather make ten rough flows in a day and kill nine of them than spend a week protecting one beautiful idea.

02

Designers should write more

Writing forces clarity. If I can't explain a design decision in one sentence, I probably don't understand the problem well enough yet.

03

Tokens over taste

Design systems should be opinionated enough to move fast and flexible enough to not feel like a cage. Tokens give you both.

04

Defaults are decisions

Every default you set is a decision you're making for millions of users. The best defaults feel invisible — that's what makes them hard to get right.

05

AI in the loop, not in the driver's seat

AI should amplify human creativity, not replace it. The best AI features feel like a helpful assistant, not an autopilot you can't override.
